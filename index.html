What is Crowd Funding ??

Crowdfunding is a method of financing a project or venture by collecting small contributions from a large number of people, typically through online platforms. This democratized funding model enables creators, entrepreneurs, or organizations to raise capital and engage a broad audience in supporting their initiatives.

What is the goal ??

The goal of crowdfunding is to raise funds from a large number of people, often through online platforms, to support a project, idea, or cause. It democratizes financing, allowing creators and entrepreneurs to access capital directly from a diverse group of backers.

BENIFITS OF CROWD FUNDING

Crowdfunding enables entrepreneurs to raise funds from a diverse group of backers, fostering financial support and community engagement. It provides access to capital without traditional financial intermediaries, validates ideas, and allows backers to contribute to projects they believe in, creating a sense of shared ownership and loyalty.

ABSTRACT

PROS :

1. Access to Capital: Easily raise funds from a large pool of individuals.
2. Market Validation: Validates product or idea's market appeal.
3. Community Engagement: Builds a supportive community around your project.
4. Diverse Backers: Attracts a diverse range of backers.
5. Low Barriers: Lowers traditional funding barriers for startups.

ABSTRACT

CONS :

1. Risk of Failure: Many crowdfunding projects do not reach their funding goals.
2. Lack of Control: Backers may influence project direction.
3. Competition: Crowded platforms make it challenging to stand out.
4. Fraud Risk: Some projects may be scams.
5. Fulfillment Issues: Delays or failure to deliver promised rewards.

HOW DOES CROWDFUNDING WORKS

Crowdfunding involves raising funds from a large number of people, typically through online platforms. Project creators set funding goals and offer rewards or incentives to backers. Individuals contribute varying amounts, collectively supporting the project. If the funding goal is met within a specified timeframe, the project receives the funds; otherwise, backers usually get their contributions refunded. Popular platforms include Kickstarter and Indiegogo, covering diverse projects from creative works to technological innovations. Crowdfunding democratizes funding, allowing creators to reach a broad audience and backers to support projects they believe in, fostering a collaborative approach to financing.
